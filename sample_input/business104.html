    Preface: I almost NEVER write reviews either way, but felt compelled to write something after my experiences here at East Bay Auto.

    About a week ago, I took my truck out of storage and decided to get back on the road. It wouldn't pass smog (at Econo Smog, who were rad to deal with). The tech their said I probably needed a tune up and it would pass no problem. So I take my ride to East Bay Auto, and Danny says that I should have an inspection rather than fork out for a full tune up, since the truck otherwise runs fine. I'm stoked at his thoughtful suggestion. I end up paying about $400 for the inspection and what turned out to be a catalytic converter replacement. A little bit pricey, but worth it since he gets me in and out the same day.

    I take it back to Econo Smog the next day. Pass! Stoked. East Bay Auto would have gotten 5 stars for this transaction, IF I didn't come back.

    A couple of days later, the truck is dripping A LOT of oil from the front. It's old, so I'm not shocked. Now I am not the smartest man, but I have decent mechanical aptitude, and I KNOW HOW TO USE GOOGLE. So I check out the forums, and folks are saying it is very common for the front oil seal to go out on this particular model.

    It's anywhere from a 30 minute to 2 hour job depending on skill level, and literally $10 in parts. Throw in another $20 for new belts because YOU HAVE TO TAKE BELTS OFF TO ACCESS THE SEAL ANYWAY.

    I'm in a hurry, and not entirely confident in taking apart my ride yet. So I call Danny up, and he says I should bring it right in. He quotes me $295 for the oil seal with labor (and tells me something to the effect of "who knows what else they might find, like the timing chain gasket is blown etc) , and AN ADDITIONAL $95 for belts.

    There is no way this adds up. I had a really good first impression from them after the cat replacement and the staff's very kind attitudes, but given this ridiculously inflated price for a relatively simple job, I doubt I'll be going back.

    I'm very disappointed. Given the number of minor repairs my truck needs, it would have been dope to just have one guy to take it to, and he could have easily made all of that money and then some off of me if he'd been honest, because even though I'm capable, I'm lazy and time is money. But I'm not an idiot. I've never had a "my" mechanic. Unfortunately, these guys ain't it.
