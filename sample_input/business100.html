    • 412 friends

    • Andi T.

    San Francisco, CA

    7/27/2007 1 photo First to Review

    The gas pumps work fine, and there's plenty of soapy water and supplies at the squeegee stations.  

    The tiny mini-mart is supplied with the basics you'd expect, as well as one of the sweetest guys around.  He helped me pick out the right motor oil, and was surprised that I'd be installing it myself ("...most times, the husband does that!").  He came running out with a funnel for me to borrow and seemed sincerely concerned that my car required 2 quarts of oil ("maybe you have a leak!").  He didn't judge me for not having the oil changed 6 months ago like I was supposed to, and I never told him that I hadn't used the dipstick and just guessed about the 2 quarts.  

    I'd go back to this 76 just for him.

    Listed in: Alameda

    I stopped by this 76 station with my co-worker last week. After pumping $60 into his Jeep, he ducked into the mini-mart for a bottle of water. When he handed over his $20 bill for a $2 purchase, the guy behind the counter refused to make change, saying "You'll have to give me something smaller." My friend says fine, and reaches for his ATM card. Guy shakes his head and points to the sign: "Credit Cards: $4 minimum purchase."

    So after spending $60 on gas, the attendant refuses to change a $20. We left with a full tank and dry throats. Won't ever go back again.

    This place is fraudulent.  I got gas there 2 days ago, filled up my car from E to Full. Gas price fluctuates from $3-$4/gal so it usually costs me around $40-50 to fill up completely.  Checked my bank account earlier and found a $100 charge from the place!  There is no way I could've filled up to $100 driving a compact with a 13 gal  tank.  I called my bank hoping to dispute the charge, but they told me unless I kept the receipt, 9 times out of 10 I would lose.   I never keep gas receipts.  The loss is only $50 so I'm just going to swallow it.  I am never getting gas here again.  

    From this $50 lesson,  I learned to always keep receipts until after the transactions have cleared through my bank.

    Union 76 station located at Webster St x Buena Vista.  I use this station when I really need gas. Otherwise, I wouldn't come here because there's limited space. There's a bus stop right there, and riders seem to think its OK to use the gas island for shade.

    Secret :  All the gas stations on Webster Street keep their prices in step with the Chevron.  If gas is $4.50/gal at Chevron, then gas will be around $4.50/gal at the Shell Station.

    Update:  8-4-08- I went there to buy gas. I went inside to buy a soda, and the old man refused to take my $20 bill.   Drop one star.
